The reality

Everyone's on their own ChatGPT. Nothing talks to anything else.

Most agencies already have people using AI - but each person is in their own silo. Their own account. Their own prompts. Their own context. Intelligence isn't being shared, so nothing compounds across the business.

Some try to solve that with prompt training. But that's treating the symptom, not the problem. The real issue isn't individual usage - it's the lack of connected infrastructure underneath.

Fragmented tools

Different people using different tools, none of them connected. No shared context, no shared intelligence.

No clear strategy

Plenty of experimentation, no roadmap. Nobody's sure what's working or what to prioritise next.

Wasted investment

Money goes into AI-powered platforms and subscriptions that nobody uses properly - or that solve the wrong problems.

Decision paralysis

The pace of change becomes an excuse to do nothing. Meanwhile, smarter competitors start building systems that actually compound.

Why now

The agencies that adapt start compounding. The ones that wait start paying for it.

There's a point where AI stops being a nice-to-have experiment and starts becoming operational leverage. Agencies that build shared context and connected workflows move faster, protect margin better, and make smarter decisions with the same team.

Agencies that keep AI fragmented don't just miss upside. They accumulate drag - more duplicated work, weaker visibility, slower handoffs, and a growing gap between what their team could do and what the business actually captures.
